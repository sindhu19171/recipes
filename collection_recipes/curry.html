Paneer Butter Masala

Ingridients Required

  • Paneer (Indian cottage cheese) - 250 grams, cubed
  • Tomatoes - 4 medium-sized, roughly chopped
  • Onion - 1 large, roughly chopped
  • Cashew nuts - 10-12 pieces
  • Ginger - 1-inch piece, chopped
  • Garlic cloves - 3-4, chopped
  • Green chili - 1, chopped (adjust according to spice preference)
  • Butter - 2-3 tablespoons
  • Red chili powder - 1 teaspoon
  • Garam masala - 1 teaspoon
  • Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) - 1 teaspoon
  • Turmeric powder - 1/2 teaspoon
  • Heavy cream - 1/2 cup
  • Salt - to taste
  • Water - as needed

Directions to prepare

Heat oil and a tablespoon of butter in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them crackle. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. Then, add chopped ginger, garlic, and green chili. Cook for a couple of minutes. Add cashew nuts and chopped tomatoes. Sauté until the tomatoes become soft and mushy. Allow the mixture to cool down a bit, then blend it into a smooth paste using a blender or food processor. You can strain the mixture through a sieve for a smoother gravy if desired. In the same pan, add a tablespoon of butter. Pour the blended tomato-onion paste back into the pan. Stir in red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, kasuri methi, sugar (if using), and salt. Cook the mixture for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add water to achieve the desired consistency for the gravy. Simmer for a few more minutes. Add the cubed paneer into the gravy. Let it simmer for 2-3 minutes. Finally, add heavy cream and mix well. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes. Garnish with a drizzle of cream or a sprinkle of chopped cilantro if desired. Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.

Enjoy your delicious Paneer Butter Masala!
